In a significant win for **access to justice**, the **Supreme Court of India** condoned a delay of **576 days** in filing a Special Leave Petition on behalf of a client from a small village in Bihar.

## The Case

The case involved a property dispute where the client, due to illiteracy and lack of local legal resources, missed the limitation period for filing an appeal. The opposing party argued that the delay was inexcusable and should result in the case being dismissed at the threshold.

## Our Argument

Our team argued that **procedural technicalities should not override substantive justice**, especially for litigants from rural backgrounds who face genuine barriers in accessing the legal system. We presented evidence of the client's circumstances and cited Supreme Court precedents that favour a liberal approach to condonation of delay when fundamental rights are involved.

## The Outcome

The Supreme Court agreed with our submissions. The delay was condoned, and the **Special Leave Petition was admitted**. The client never left his village — we handled everything remotely, from drafting to filing to arguing the condonation application.
