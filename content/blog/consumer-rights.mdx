Every time you buy a product or pay for a service, you become a consumer. The law gives you specific rights to protect you from unfair trade practices, defective goods, and deficient services. Yet, most Indians don't know how powerful these rights are.

**The 6 Basic Consumer Rights in India**

The Consumer Protection Act, 2019, recognises six fundamental rights for every consumer.

First is the **Right to Safety**. You have the right to be protected against goods and services that are hazardous to life and property. This applies to everything – from electrical appliances and packaged food to medicines and vehicles.

Second is the **Right to be Informed**. A seller must give you complete information about the product – its ingredients, price, expiry date, warranty terms, and any risks. If someone hides important facts or makes false claims, they are violating this right.

Third is the **Right to Choose**. No one can force you to buy a specific brand or product. You are free to compare, ask questions, and pick what suits you best. Tying a product to another or refusing to sell unless you buy a bundle is illegal.

Fourth is the **Right to be Heard**. If you have a genuine grievance, the law guarantees that your complaint will be heard and considered. You can approach consumer forums at the district, state, or national level without even hiring a lawyer.

Fifth is the **Right to Seek Redressal**. This means you can claim compensation, replacement, or refund for defective products or deficient services. You can also demand punitive damages if the trader's conduct was especially wrongful.

Sixth is the **Right to Consumer Awareness**. The government and consumer organisations run campaigns to educate people about their rights. Being aware is the first step toward protecting yourself.

**What Counts as a Valid Consumer Complaint**

You can file a complaint for: defective goods, deficient services, overcharging, unfair trade practices, misleading advertisements, and hazardous products. Even a delay in delivery or refusal to honour a warranty is a valid ground.

**Where and How to File a Complaint**

You don't always need a lawyer. You can file a complaint yourself before the appropriate consumer forum.

For claims up to Rs 50 lakh – approach the District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission. For claims between Rs 50 lakh and Rs 2 crore – approach the State Commission. For claims above Rs 2 crore – approach the National Commission (NCDRC) in New Delhi.

You can file your complaint in the place where you reside or where the seller operates. A simple written application with details of the problem, the relief you want, and copies of relevant documents is enough. There is a nominal fee based on the claim amount.

**Important Points to Remember**

Always keep bills, receipts, warranty cards, and screenshots of online purchases. These are your strongest evidence. Send a written notice to the seller before filing a formal complaint – many disputes settle at this stage.

You must file the complaint within two years from the date the problem arose. Don't delay.
